## 2. Communities & Groups to Engage In

### r/ScienceBasedParenting — [reddit.com/r/ScienceBasedParenting](https://www.reddit.com/r/ScienceBasedParenting/)
- **Size/activity:** ~259K members; very active discussion threads on sleep, toddler development, and routines, with a culture that favors sourced, evidence-based answers.
- **How to add value:** This community is skeptical of fluffy marketing but receptive to "here's what the research says, and here's what we tried" posts. Josh could genuinely contribute by discussing the developmental rationale behind specific activities (e.g., why predictable bedtime routines reduce toddler resistance) — credibility built here would carry weight precisely because the audience double-checks claims.

### Taking Cara Babies Community (Facebook) — [facebook.com/TakingCaraBabies](https://www.facebook.com/TakingCaraBabies/)
- **Size/activity:** 335K+ Facebook followers, part of a broader community of 2M+ families; covers sleep training for newborns through toddlers (ages 2–4 are explicitly in scope).
- **How to add value:** This audience has already bought into "structured routines reduce bedtime battles" — Josh's Good Nights 🌙 pack is a natural complement to sleep-training methods rather than a competitor. Genuine participation (sharing what worked once routines were established, not pitching) could plant seeds with a highly routine-motivated audience.

### The Screen-Free Parenting Community (Facebook) — [facebook.com/groups/127311545931274](https://www.facebook.com/groups/127311545931274/)

## 3. Podcasts & Blogs Worth Pitching

### Startup Dad — [Adam Fishman's podcast](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/startup-dad/id1693312339)
- **Audience focus:** Interviews with executives, founders, and entrepreneurs who are also fathers, covering how they balance building companies with raising kids.
- **Why the founder story resonates:** Josh's arc — write activities by hand for his daughters in 2004, pitch Scholastic, get rejected, shelve it for 25 years, then finally build the app — is a "long game" founder story that this show's audience (dad-founders, generally) would find both relatable and motivating. - **How to pitch:** Reach out via the podcast's contact links on Apple Podcasts/its website; lead with the specific narrative beats (the notebook, the Scholastic rejection letter if it still exists, the 25-year gap) rather than the app's feature set.

- **How to pitch:** Look for a contact/collaboration link on goodnightfox.com; pitch a short guest post or newsletter mention framed around shared mission (screen-free toddler activities), with the founder story as the narrative hook.

## 5. One Opportunity That Surprised Me

While researching toy/product founder stories for podcast pitch angles, I came across **People of Play's "Global Inventor Pitch & Innovation Conference,"** part of POP Week / the Chicago Toy & Game Fair (CHITAG), returning to Chicago **November 5–8, 2026**. It's described as the largest toy and game inventor conference in the world — independent inventors pitch directly to companies like Hasbro, Mattel, LEGO, and Spin Master, and the event gets meaningful trade and consumer press coverage every year.

**Why this matters for Everyday Adventures:** Josh's origin story is *literally* the premise of this entire event — an independent person with a notebook full of kid-activity ideas, pitching a major company (Scholastic), and getting passed on. While Everyday Adventures is an app rather than a physical toy, the conference's orbit (inventor-story press coverage, the "Today" show and trade press that cover POP Week, the New Toy & Game Inventors Track) represents a concentrated cluster of journalists and media outlets who are *primed* to find "rejected decades ago, finally launched" stories compelling. Even without exhibiting, this is worth flagging as a press-outreach moment in early November — pitching Josh's story to the same writers covering POP Week could land Everyday Adventures coverage by riding the seasonal "inventor story" news cycle rather than competing for attention in a generic app-launch news cycle.
